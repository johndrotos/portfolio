Summer 2024

Embedding Matrix Evolution in Vision Transformers

Last summer, I worked at the Graphics Imaging and Light Measurement Lab at Columbia University with Professor Corey Toler-Franklin and a graduate student team developing a novel vision transformer architecture for tumor detection in medical slides. My role this summer was to run an experiment to better understand how transformers learned during the fine tuning process when trained on our medical data, and I did so by generating comparisons of the embedding matrices before and after fine-tuning. My experiment dissected a technology that is typically regarded as a black-box, and my results provided the team with a much better understanding of the inner workings of the models we are attempting to improve upon. I am happily continuing my work with the lab this fall.
